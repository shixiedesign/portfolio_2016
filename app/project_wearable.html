Wearable Privacy Device

// A new take on privacy in the age of wearable technology

A metaphor for internet anonymity, the Wearable Privacy Device is a curious speculation on the behaviors to expect if people are allowed to hide / reveal their own identities fluidly in real life, without even have to logout-login like how one might do so online. The device proposes a new form of anonymity that can be switched on anywhere, any time. When the incognito mode is turned on, the device renders the area of the wearers eyes into a black bar, making the wearer instantly "unidentifiable".

Year 2015
Client Personal
Role Designer

The Concept

The project critics the phenomenon of trolling, when a member of the society chooses to disregard established social contracts with others when his or her identity is concealed, and action unaccountable. The problem is the damage done in the virtual world is still the reality of someone else. The device aims to allow people to fully grasp the power and gravity of trolling by enabling trolling in real life. The project is inspired by works of Brian Kane.

Device Context Awareness

The wearable device is fitted with Wifi and proximity detection, which in combination with the wearer's phone's GPS, allowing the device to be context aware to some extent, and automatically switch the anonymity on and off depending on customizable privacy preferences.

Censor Bars

The initial experiment to evoke the look of a censor bar is by repurposing an iphone 4-way privacy screen. The result is surprisingly effective, and the wearer has fluid control to reveal or conceal the eyes by simply turning one's face away. The problem is the privacy screen actually diffract the light dramatically, causing rainbow vision. It is really fun at first, but quickly makes the wearer dizzy and nauseous.
